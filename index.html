The LUTA Hand: Development of a Lightweight Underactuated Three-fingered Robotic Hand for Adaptive Grasping

Seunghwan Byun1, Seongkyeong Moon2, Eunho Sung1, Seungbin You1, Yong-Lae Park1,*, Jaeheung Park1,*
1Seoul National University
2Samsung Electronics
International Journal of Control, Automation, and Systems (IJCAS) 2025
Paper Video Code

The LUTA Hand demonstrates robust adaptive grasping using a single servomotor and shared tendon-routing. It passively conforms to objects of varying sizes and shapes without independent joint control.

Abstract

Robotic hands are central to a wide range of manipulation tasks, enabling human-like dexterity and adaptability. However, achieving such functionality with minimal actuation and reduced mechanical complexity remains a key challenge.

This study presents a lightweight, underactuated three-fingered robotic hand that incorporates tendon-driven actuation and compliant joint structures. A shared tendon-routing scheme enables synchronized finger flexion with a single actuator, while passive restoring forces are provided by rolling contact joints and elastic ligaments, removing the need for antagonistic actuation. All actuation components are embedded within the hand, resulting in a compact and portable configuration.

Experimental evaluations demonstrate consistent finger motion, passive adaptation to varying object geometries, and the ability to perform multiple types of grasps with an underactuated architecture.

Adaptive Grasping Mechanism

The LUTA Hand achieves adaptive grasping through its single, underactuated, tendon-driven design. A single tendon forms a continuous loop sequentially linking all three fingers. When one finger contacts an object and is kinematically constrained, the motor continues to rotate, redirecting the remaining tendon excursion to the non-contacting fingers.

This sequential contact behavior enables the hand to passively conform to various shapes and sizes. It allows the natural emergence of both pinch and power grasp configurations without any active reconfiguration.

Passive Pretension Mechanism

To enable finger extension without antagonistic actuators, the hand incorporates a passive mechanism comprising elastic extension tendons and rolling contact joint ligaments. The lateral latex ligaments (3M Unitek "Bummer") are deliberately positioned with a 3 mm posterior offset from the center of rotation.

This specific offset establishes a moment arm at the neutral position, producing a restorative torque that returns the joint to neutral while maintaining lateral stability and limiting parasitic yaw during flexion.
